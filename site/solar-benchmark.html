Nine systems. One impossible brief.

Solar Bench separates the what from the why. Nine models received the same sprawling brief. We score whether each build delivered what it literally requested—and whether it created the experience the brief was trying to describe.

Explicit execution · the what Did it deliver the requested product?

Required bodies, science, controls, rendering, accessibility, documentation and verification.

Implicit intent · the why Did it understand the experience?

A credible, cinematic simulation that feels like a premium game and stays compelling on a second monitor.

Why equal weight? A perfect checklist with no believable world has missed the assignment. A beautiful globe with dead controls is not a finished product. Neither failure mode can hide inside the other axis.

The verdict

Fable wins by finishing the whole thought.

Kimi K3 and Sonnet 5 each beat it somewhere important. Kimi makes the simulation feel most continuously alive; Sonnet makes Earth look most like Earth. But Fable is the only build that joins a complete system, high-end interaction, cinematic staging, scientific depth, documentation and browser verification without a serious structural hole.

The fair top-three gap is where each model accepts compromise: Fable invents geography, Kimi invents surface data, and Sonnet leaves camera direction and browser reliability unfinished. Fable's compromise is least damaging across the full brief.

04 · Scoring method

How the two-axis score works

Every criterion is scored from 0 (absent) to 5 (fully delivered and verified). Optional audio and extras are recorded but excluded from the headline result.

Explicit execution · A

Sixteen required criteria

Audits the literal brief: rendering stack, architecture, catalogue completeness, science, camera, zoom, visuals, UI, controls, accessibility, performance engineering, tests and deliverables.

The question: did the model build what was requested?

Implicit intent · B

Ten experience probes

Tests the meaning behind the list: resourcefulness, a believable Earth, cinematic arrivals, game feel, honesty, substance, scientific soul, idle life, scale and hard-body detail.

The question: did the model understand why it was requested?

Equal-weight result

Neither axis can dominate

Each subtotal is normalized to 10, then the two axes are averaged. This makes experience quality as consequential as checklist coverage.

Explicit = A ÷ 8
Implicit = B ÷ 5
Overall = (Explicit + Implicit) ÷ 2
